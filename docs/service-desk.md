# Service Desk Operations

## Purpose
To provide a centralized point of contact for all IT-related incidents, service requests, and technical support needs.

The service desk ensures consistent service delivery and effective communication between IT and users.

---

## Service Desk Functions
- Incident management
- Service request fulfillment
- User support and guidance
- Access requests
- IT communication and updates
- Knowledge management

---

## Ticket Lifecycle

### 1. Ticket Logging
Tickets may be logged via:
- Email
- Portal
- Phone call
- Chat

Each ticket must include:
- Issue description
- Affected system
- Impact and urgency
- User details

---

### 2. Categorization & Prioritization
Tickets are categorized by:
- Incident or service request
- System affected
- Severity level

Priority is determined by:
- Business impact
- Number of users affected
- Urgency

---

### 3. Assignment
- Assigned to appropriate support level
- Escalated if beyond scope
- SLA timelines applied

---

### 4. Investigation & Resolution
- Troubleshooting performed
- Fix applied and tested
- User informed of progress

---

### 5. Closure
- Confirmation from user
- Resolution documented
- Knowledge base updated if applicable

---

## Severity Levels
- **Critical** – System-wide outage
- **High** – Major functionality impacted
- **Medium** – Partial disruption
- **Low** – Minor issue or request

---

## Service Desk KPIs
- First Response Time
- Mean Time to Resolution (MTTR)
- SLA Compliance
- First Contact Resolution
- Customer Satisfaction (CSAT)

---

## Communication Standards
- Clear and professional responses
- Regular status updates
- Non-technical language for end users
- Proper documentation of all actions

---

## Tools Commonly Used
- ServiceNow
- Zendesk
- Jira Service Management
- Freshservice
- Internal knowledge base

---

## Outcome
A well-run service desk:
- Improves user experience
- Reduces downtime
- Increases productivity
- Builds trust in IT services
